FAQ

The questions I get asked every week.

How fast can we start?
Usually within two weeks. The Assessment can happen on a single day once we find one that works. The Implementation Sprint is typically 3–4 weeks end-to-end. Retainers start the first business day of the month.
Do we need to buy new software?
Probably not. For most small firms, the existing subscriptions (Microsoft 365, Clio, one AI chat tool like Claude or ChatGPT) cover eighty-plus percent of useful workflows. If we recommend something new, we'll show you the math on why.
What if we're already using AI and just need a second opinion?
That's a real engagement — usually scoped as a shorter version of the Assessment. Email me and we'll figure it out.
Do you work with firms outside the US?
Yes, for advisory and retainer work. Implementation engagements work best with firms in US or Canadian time zones for practical scheduling reasons.
What about privilege and client confidentiality?
Every workflow is designed to respect existing privilege rules, client-matter isolation, and your compliance requirements. I'll sign an NDA before the Assessment if that's helpful. I don't use client data for anything other than your engagement.
Do you offer payment plans?
The Assessment and Sprint are invoiced at start. The Retainer is month-to-month. If an unusual structure helps your firm, ask — within reason, we'll work it out.
Do you speak at events?
Yes. Bar associations, firm management conferences, partner retreats, CLEs. Priced separately. Email me with the date and format.
What's the biggest mistake firms make with AI?
Buying another tool before they've actually used the one they already pay for. It's free to try what's on your desk first.

Still deciding which tier fits?

Twenty minutes on a call and we'll figure it out. Free, and I'll tell you honestly if your firm isn't ready for AI yet.
