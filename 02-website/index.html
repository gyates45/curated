The problem

Most AI tools sold to law firms are overpriced chatbots with a legal skin.

You've already paid for Microsoft 365. You probably have Clio. You might have ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini. That's a stack that can handle the majority of what "legal AI" vendors will sell you for $400 a seat.

The question isn't which tool to buy next. It's which tool you already own that you haven't actually used yet.

What I do

Three things. Done well. In that order.

01 · Productivity

Hours back, every week.

We find the places where your team is spending time on work that shouldn't be billable — intake summaries, drafting form letters, meeting notes, status updates — and we automate them. No new software. Just the tools you have, used correctly.

02 · Revenue

Higher-value matters.

Faster intake-to-engagement. New service lines your firm couldn't profitably offer before. Matters you'd have turned down because the margins were too thin — now viable, because half the work is done by tools you already own.

03 · Client delight

The polish that compounds.

Faster response times. Clearer client communications. Monthly status notes that actually land. The client-facing layer that separates a firm you recommend from a firm you tolerate.
